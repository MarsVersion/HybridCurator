About Me

Curating has long been my approach to engaging with others—through exhibitions, conversations, and collaborative projects spanning Europe and Asia. From founding Diskurs Berlin to working with institutions like Kunstmuseum Bochum, the Asia Triennial Manchester, and the Mediations Biennale in Poznań, I've focused on building thoughtful, inclusive platforms for artistic dialogue.

Over time, another challenge became clear: the art world lacked digital tools that reflected its own complexity. Discovery was slow. Too many voices remained unseen.

In 2024, I stepped back from curating to learn programming. What began as curiosity became a new kind of practice. With the help of AI tools—and over a year of focused study—I created Busy Mars, an AI-powered platform that helps curators, artists, and institutions connect more easily.

This site, hybridcurator.com, brings together both sides of my work: curatorial thinking and technical experimentation. It's grounded in the belief that curating today also means building systems for visibility, care, and connection.

I'm open to collaborations, dialogues, and creative experiments—feel free to get in touch.
